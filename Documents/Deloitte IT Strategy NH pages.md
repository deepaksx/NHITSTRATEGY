• The **people function** is severely constrained by a **limited staff size**, which leads to over-burdened employees and a significant **risk of single points of failure** across critical IT roles.

• There is a notable **absence of employee growth plans** and formalized career pathing, which prevents the IT team from aligning personal development with organizational needs.

• The organization currently lacks defined **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)** and performance factors, making objective resource assessment impossible.

• Existing **policies and procedures** are inconsistently applied and monitored, which directly reduces their effectiveness in governing daily operations.

• A critical gap exists in **IT budgeting and cost management** due to a lack of structured tracking tools and formal processes.

• The absence of a **documented cybersecurity framework** leads to an unstructured approach to security and compliance, increasing operational risk.

• There is currently **no budget allocated** specifically for professional certifications or skill development for the existing IT workforce.

• The **current organizational structure** does not provide a mechanism for tracking the technical performance of resources against business goals.

• The lack of a **formal governance structure** aligned with international standards like **ISO 27001 and ISO 38000** is a primary process weakness.

• Overlapping roles mean team members are often "double-hatted," performing tasks outside their core competencies without adequate support.

• **Leading Level**: Represented by the highest level of maturity where technology and processes are fully optimized across the group.
