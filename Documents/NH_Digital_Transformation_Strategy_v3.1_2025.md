### Why Digital Transformation

**Four Strategic Pillars:**

#### IT Governance & Compliance
Align IT governance with holding company's vision and business strategy. Ensure compliance with regulatory and industry standards (ISO 27001, NIST, GDPR, etc.). Implement robust cybersecurity and risk management frameworks to protect business continuity.

#### Digital Transformation & Innovation
Leverage cloud, AI, and automation to enhance efficiency and scalability. Foster a culture of continuous innovation to support new business models and market expansion. Enable seamless digital integration across subsidiaries to drive synergies and competitive advantage.

#### Data & AI for Decision-Making
Implement enterprise-wide data governance to ensure accuracy, security, and compliance. Utilize AI-driven analytics to optimize business performance and customer experiences. Enable real-time data-driven decision-making to support business growth and market positioning.

#### ERP Modernization
Modernize business applications to support business expansion. Enhance agility and operational efficiency.

#### SWOT Analysis

**STRENGTHS**
- **People**: Small team allows for flexible role coverage and adaptability; Team members possess diverse skillsets
- **Process**: Process improvement through ISO27001 program; Understanding of current operation challenges
- **Technology**: Potential to adapt through introduction of new technologies; Familiarity with existing systems

**WEAKNESSES**
- **People**: Missing Key Roles and Functions; Limited staff (single point of failure); No KPI and performance factors defined
- **Process**: Operational Risks due to lack of structured processes; Lack of formal governance structure; No shared IT budget
- **Technology**: Minimal system integration and automation; Limited automation leads to time-consuming manual processes

**OPPORTUNITIES**
- **People**: Upskilling through training programs; Hiring additional staff or outsource tasks; Leverage automation tools
- **Process**: Formalization of budgeting; Opportunity for governance alignment with business goals
- **Technology**: Implementation of automation tools; Enhancement of internal customer experience

**THREATS**
- **People**: Absence of dedicated roles for critical functions; Current skillset unable to support transformation
- **Process**: Potential resistance to change; Potential disruptions during implementation
- **Technology**: Delayed adoption impacting performance; Competitive disadvantage; Tech advancements may outpace infrastructure

#### High-Level Capability Gaps

**PEOPLE**
- Organizational structure lacks standardization
- Critical functions (Business Analysis, PMO, Cybersecurity) missing or inadequately defined
- Roles and responsibilities not clearly defined
- Designations don't align with responsibilities
- No structured methodology to assess skill gaps
- Multiple roles handled by same individuals (single point of failure)
- No career growth and succession planning framework

**PROCESS**
- Policies developed but inconsistently applied and monitored
- No tools/processes to track IT budgeting and cost management
- No documented IT and security governance framework
- Absence of formal cybersecurity and IT governance
- No budget for training, certifications, or skill development
- No KPIs or performance tracking mechanism
- No structured career development or certification path

**TECHNOLOGY**
- Lack of foundational business applications (HRMS, Service Management, Audit management)
- Automation capabilities highly limited or nonexistent
- Limited system integration leads to excessive manual processes
- Strong dependency on third-party vendors for cybersecurity functions

### Appendix C: ERP Strategy Details

**HRMS Gap:**
- Inconsistent HR processes impede workforce planning
- Data fragmentation limits talent management
- Restricts ability to attract and retain top talent

**Finance EPM/Treasury Gap:**
- Limited financial planning and analysis capabilities
- Restricts informed strategic decisions
- Inadequate financial oversight and risk management
- Exposes organization to financial volatility

**Procurement Gap:**
- Inefficiencies in procurement processes
- Higher costs and reduced agility
- Hindered strategic sourcing
- Reduced ability to respond to supply chain disruptions

**GRC Gap:**
- Increased risk of non-compliance
- Ineffective risk management
- Potential reputational damage
- Possible financial penalties

#### ERP Implementation Approach

**Guiding Principles:**
